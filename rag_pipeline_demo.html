Interviewer: "Putting the judge at the end adds latency and timeout risk — how do you handle that?"

Answer: "That's exactly why I used LangGraph. The judge isn't a one-off call — it's a dedicated Node inside a cyclic state graph. On failure, instead of returning a bad response, the graph routes back to the generation node (self-correction) or triggers a fallback model — completely transparent to the end-user."
